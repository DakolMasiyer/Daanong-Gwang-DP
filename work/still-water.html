Still Water is a personal project — a short non-narrative film about stillness, light, and patience. Shot over five days in the Scottish Highlands during late autumn, it was made with a minimal crew of two.

The project began as a personal exploration of what happens when a cinematographer removes narrative entirely and films purely in response to light. No script, no schedule — only conditions.
