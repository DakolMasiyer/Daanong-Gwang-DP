A feature drama set across the Niger Delta, Between Two Rivers traces the lives of two families living on opposite banks of the same river. The film was shot across a 38-day schedule spanning dry and flood seasons.

The visual approach draws on the region's extreme light conditions — blinding noon reflections off the water, deep blue pre-dawn hours, and the amber warmth of kerosene interiors — to anchor each scene emotionally in place and time.
