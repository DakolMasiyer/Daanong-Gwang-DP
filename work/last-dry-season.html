The Last Dry Season is an hour-long documentary examining water scarcity across three communities in the Lake Chad Basin. Shot over two dry seasons, the film documents both the landscape in crisis and the communities adapting to it.

The visual approach prioritises proximity — handheld, close to subjects, unhurried. The camera never observes from a distance what it can observe from within.
