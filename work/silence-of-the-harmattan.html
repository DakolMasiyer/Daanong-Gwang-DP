Shot over six weeks across the Sahel corridor, Silence of the Harmattan follows three communities living at the edge of an expanding desert. The film explores the relationship between land, memory, and survival through intimate vérité photography.

The cinematography draws on the natural light conditions of the region — the diffuse white sky of the harmattan wind, long golden hours, and the hard midday contrast of open terrain — to build a visual language rooted in place.
