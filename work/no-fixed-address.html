Shot across four nights in central Lagos, No Fixed Address follows a young woman navigating homelessness in the city. The film was conceived as a single unbroken visual essay — observational, non-interventionist, and deliberately underlit.

The cinematography leans into available light from street sources — sodium vapour, neon, LED signage — to build a fragmented, nocturnal visual language that mirrors the protagonist's state of uncertainty.
